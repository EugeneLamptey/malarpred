MalarPred is a cutting-edge machine learning-based web tool designed to help researchers to identify small molecule inhibitors targeting the AMA-1/RON2 invasion complex. This interaction is crucial for tight junction formation, a key step in red blood cell invasion by Plasmodium falciparum merozoites. By disrupting this essential pathway, MalarPred facilitates the discovery of novel therapeutic candidates to combat malaria. Powered by robust machine learning models, including Random Forest, Gradient Boost Machines, and CatBoost, MalarPred has been meticulously trained on high-quality data to provide accurate and reliable predictions. This tool offers a transformative resource for advancing antimalarial drug development and accelerating global efforts to mitigate malaria.
